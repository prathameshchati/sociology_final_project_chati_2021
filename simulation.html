The Occipital Lobe is the part of the brain responsible for our vision, including depth perception, perceiving motion, identifying color, and recognizing object features. Damage in this area often causes side effects such as hallucinations, blindness, inability to recognize objects, lack of depth perception, and trouble perceiving our environment.

Our brain is separated into two hemispheres (right and left lobes). The Corpus Callosum is the bundle of nervse that essentially bridges the left and right lobes of the brain, enabling rapid communication between the two lobes. Different sides of our brain have varying functions, therefore, it is vital that the two sides of the brain are able to communicate rapidly and efficiently.

The Thalamus is located near the core center of the brain, with the Hypothalamus sitting right below the Thalamus. Although named similarly, the Thalamus and Hypothalamus serve distinct functions. The Thalamus is responsible for maintaining a state of consciousness and alertness. Broadly, the Thalamus has an overarching role in processing sensory and motor signals.
